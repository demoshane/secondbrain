## Warnings

### W-01: CAP-08 and CAP-09 Not Tagged in SUMMARY.md Frontmatter

**Severity:** Warning (documentation gap only)
**Finding:** CAP-08 (sb-* commands invocable from Claude Code/Cowork) and CAP-09 (CLAUDE.md
proactive capture instructions) are marked Complete in REQUIREMENTS.md traceability table (Phase 6)
and implemented in `engine/ai.py` / `~/.claude/CLAUDE.md`, but no Phase 6 SUMMARY.md frontmatter
lists them under `requirements-completed`. 48 of 50 requirements have explicit SUMMARY.md
coverage; these 2 are covered only by the REQUIREMENTS.md traceability table.
**Impact:** No functionality missing. Cross-reference shows 48/50 via SUMMARY.md; 50/50 via
REQUIREMENTS.md.
**Resolution (optional):** Add `CAP-08` and `CAP-09` to a Phase 6 SUMMARY.md
`requirements-completed` list.

### W-02: CAP-07 Templates Not Enforced at Capture Runtime

**Severity:** Warning (partial implementation)
**Finding:** CAP-07 (notes use consistent Markdown templates per content type) is satisfied at the
template-file level — templates exist in `.meta/templates/` and are seeded by `init_brain.py`.
However, `capture_note()` does not call `render_template()` at runtime; templates are advisory, not
enforced.
**Impact:** Notes captured via `sb-capture` do not automatically inherit template structure.
**Resolution:** Wire `render_template()` into `capture_note()` in `engine/capture.py`. Deferred to
v2 scope.

## Tech Debt

### TD-01: Phase 11 GDPR Expanded Scope Not Reflected in REQUIREMENTS.md IDs

**Finding:** Phase 11 expanded GDPR-02, GDPR-03, and GDPR-06 beyond their original Phase 2/5 scope
(adding `sb-export`, runtime `anonymize()`, first-run consent prompt). REQUIREMENTS.md traceability
table still lists these IDs under their original phases. No new IDs (GDPR-07, GDPR-08, GDPR-09)
were added.
**Impact:** Traceability understates Phase 11 delivery. Functionality is implemented and tested.
**Resolution (optional):** Add GDPR-07/08/09 to REQUIREMENTS.md for Phase 11 capabilities.

### TD-02: Shell Executable Registration Requires Manual Re-install After New Entry Points

**Finding:** Adding new `[project.scripts]` entries (as done in Phases 11/12 for `sb-anonymize`,
`sb-update-memory`, `sb-export`) requires running `uv tool install --editable .` again to register
shell executables. Tests verify importability but not shell-path registration.
**Impact:** Risk only when adding future entry points without re-running install.
**Resolution (optional):** Document re-install step in INSTALL.md or add check to
`bootstrap.py --dev`.
